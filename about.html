Tools Used

Vue.js is an open-source JavaScript framework for building user interfaces and beneficial for building single-page applications. Vue was created by Evan You, whom worked for Google using AnguarJS. His inspiration to build the framework came from taking parts of Angular that he really like about it and building a lightweight framework out of it.

OpenAQ is the world's first open, real-time and historical air quality platform, aggregating government-measured and research-grade data - entirely open-source. The Open AQ API enables previously impossible science, impact policy and empower the public to fight air pollution through open data, open-source tools, and cooperation.

Leaflet is the leading open-source JavaScript library for mobile-friendly interactive maps. Weighing just about 38 KB of JS, it has all the mapping features most developers ever need. Leaflet is designed with simplicity, performance and usability in mind.

Nominatim is a search engine for OpenStreetMap data. You may search for a name or address (forward search) or look up data by its geographic coordinate (reverse search). Each result comes with a link to a details page where you can inspect what data about the object is saved in the database and investigate how the address of the object has been computed.

Interesting Findings

  • Los Angeles has low CO ppm even though there is a very large amout of cars in that city.
  • Minneapolis has very low levels of every single type of air polutant that was measured in the past 30 days.
  • Honolulu recorded 0 ppm of so2 for the past 30 consecutive days as of 4/25/2019
  • Delhi had a peak pm25 measurement of 519 µg/m³ over the past 30 days on 4/15/2019. This is categorized as hazardous
  • The University of Minnesota Duluth recorded extremely low levels of o3 throughout the duration of March/April. Go Minnesota!
